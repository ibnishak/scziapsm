Dr. Umesh Sinha

Dr. Umesh Sinha, currently Associate Professor, Department of Community Medicine, GMC Ratlam, is a distinguished medical professional with over 17 years of teaching experience in Community Medicine. He completed his undergraduate degree from Gandhi Medical College, Bhopal and MD in Preventive and Social Medicine from MGM Medical College, Indore. Since 2018, he has been the Associate Professor of Department of Community Medicine at GMC Ratlam. Dr. Sinha has an extensive research portfolio, with notable projects under WHO, UNICEF, and the Clinton Foundation, focusing on childhood morbidity, rural health, and PPTCT services. He has presented papers at national conferences and published over 15 research articles in reputed journals, covering topics like breastfeeding, hypertension, and tuberculosis. His thesis on the utilization of untied funds under NRHM reflects his commitment to improving public health systems. A passionate and effable educator and researcher, Dr. Sinha has participated in numerous workshops, seminars, and training programs, including those by ICMR, WHO, and UNDP. His contributions to health research and education continue to impact medical training and community health outcomes.

Dr. Lokendra Singh Kot

With over 11 years of dedicated experience in applied statistics, Dr. Lokendra Kot serves as a Demonstrator cum Statistician at Government Medical College, Ratlam. He holds an M.Sc. in Statistics from School of Studies in Statistics, Vikram University, Ujjain, and earned his Ph.D. from the School of Economics, Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya, Indore. Dr. Kot blends academic rigor with a deep passion for holistic well-being. A committed practitioner of yoga and meditation, he believes in the integration of mental clarity and physical health to enhance professional and personal life. His interdisciplinary approach and calm demeanor make him a valued mentor and collaborator in both research and teaching.
